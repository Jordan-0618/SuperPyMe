What Questions Did We Plan on Answering?

And How Would We Do It?

1. With fast food franchises on every corner, how can a prospective franchise owner determine the best location to open a new storefront? By analyzing twiiter sentiment based on location, we can determine which cities have the most positive feelings towards certain chains
2. Is it possible to determine potential location success through trends on twitter? Scrape through thousands of tweets and analyze which cities have the mose positive feelings towards certain chains
3. Are there other factors that could contribute to sentiment for a particular franchise, or for determining the most ideal location to build? Determine if other factors such as time of year, per capita income, or other demographics can affect public opinion on fast food chains

Identifying the Problem

There is a fast food restaurant on almost every block you see, however, nearly half of all franchies will be closed only 5 years after they are opened. What leads some stores to succeed and others to fail? Is there as way to look at this problem in a different way than it has been studied previously?

Corporations spend about $800 Million a year on focus group research, but what if access to public opinion is already readily available to be analyzed? We will use the sentiment of twitter users to find the cities that are most positive towards specific chains. Hoping they would be more receptive to new branches of certain chains opening up.

Specifically we looked at McDonalds, Subway, and Starbucks, as they are the three most populus chains in the US. We also kept the scope of our scraping to specifically cities within California with a population of over 75,000.

What is Twitter Sentiment?

Natural language processing (NLP) is a process that allows a machine learning program to break down tweets into smaller chunks that are more easily understood by the computer. Once all the segments are analyzed the text of each tweet is given a overall score of polarity ranging from -1 to 1.
